Event Criteria and Stages

About the Event

The Hackathon game is a competitive event where teams collaborate to develop innovative solutions to real-world problems. Participants will work on various challenges over a 24-hour period, and their solutions will be evaluated by a panel of judges.

Participation Criteria
  • Participants must be between 18-35 years old.
  • Teams should consist of 2-4 members.
  • Each team must have at least one developer and one designer.
  • Participants should have basic knowledge of programming and problem-solving skills.
  • Teams can bring their own laptops and tools but internet access will be provided.
Event Stages
  1. Stage 1 - Idea Submission: Teams will submit a short pitch of their project idea at the start of the event. The judges will review the ideas for feasibility and creativity.
  2. Stage 2 - Development: Once the ideas are approved, teams will begin working on the prototype or solution over the next 24 hours. Mentors will be available to provide guidance.
  3. Stage 3 - Presentation: Teams will present their final product or prototype in front of the judging panel. Each team will have 10 minutes to showcase their solution.
  4. Stage 4 - Judging and Awards: The judges will evaluate the presentations based on creativity, technical execution, and potential impact. Winners will be announced during the closing ceremony.
Judging Criteria
  • Innovation: How unique and original is the idea?
  • Technical Execution: How well is the solution implemented? Is it functional?
  • Impact: Does the solution address the problem effectively? What is the potential impact?
